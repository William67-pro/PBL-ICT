Introduction

In Yangon, pollution has become a growing concern due to rapid urbanization and increasing population. Traffic congestion contributes heavily to air pollution, especially from older vehicles and diesel engines that release harmful fumes. Improper waste disposal and plastic litter also affect streets, drainage systems, and nearby waterways during the rainy season. In addition, industrial activities around the city add to both air and water pollution. These challenges impact public health and highlight the need for better waste management, cleaner transport options, and stronger environmental protection efforts.

📘 What is Pollution?

Pollution is the introduction of harmful substances into the environment. These pollutants damage the air, water, and land, affecting human health and wildlife. Pollution is mostly caused by human activities such as transportation, industries, and waste disposal.

🌫 Types of Pollution

Air Pollution

the contamination of the atmosphere by harmful gases

Water Pollution

the contamination of water bodies (such as oceans, lakes, rivers, and groundwater) by harmful substances

Land or Soil Pollution

the degradation of the Earth's land surfaces caused by the accumulation of solid waste

Plastic Pollution

the accumulation of synthetic plastic waste in the environment
